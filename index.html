What is OpenICE?

OpenICE is an initiative to create a community implementation of the ASTM F2761-09 standard for the Integrated Clinical Environment. It is intended to inform the design of future parts of the ICE standard. The initiative encompasses not only software implementation of those functionalities described in the standard but also an architecture for a wider clinical ecosystem that incorporates seamless connectivity to increase patient safety. OpenICE seeks to provide a framework for the inclusion of healthcare devices and systems into the broader Internet of Things and Industrial Internet efforts.

Helpful information

ICE Standard F2761

ICE Standard F2761 - "Essential safety requirements for equipment comprising the patient-centric integrated clinical environment (ICE) — Part 1: General requirements and conceptual model" is the theoretical foundation of the OpenICE prototype. ICE-compliant architectures are intended to enable comprehensive medical device data acquisition for the EMR, and the integration of medical devices to enable real-time decision support, safety interlocks, and closed-loop control. The ICE standardization activity was initiated in 2006 by ASTM International, committee F29, based on foundational work performed by the MD PnP program and collaborators.
OMG DDS Portal

The Object Management Group's Data Distribution Service (DDS) is a standardized publish-subscribe middleware for near-real-time communication in distributed systems. DDS is used as the underlying technology to facilitate medical device and application communication. The base standard has been implemented by PrismTech, Real-Time Innovations and the OpenDDS project. For an overview of MD PnP's use of DDS, checkout this slideshare.
