OpenICE Applications

Getting Started

This tutorial contains content that is not displayed on mobile browsers. Please use a desktop/laptop browser to take advantage of all images.

This guide will assist you in using and understanding the OpenICE Applications ("apps"). Currently, all of these apps are experimental and are not used in clinical settings. Once you start the ICE Supervisor, a list of apps will appear on the left side. To open the app, click on the image. To exit the app and go back to the ICE Supervisor, click the Exit App button. The OpenICE application will close you x out of an app.

Before running an app, you should assign devices to a patient and select that patient. For instructions on connecting devices, please go to the Device Adapter Setup and User Manual page. On the bottom of the ICE Supervisor, click the dropdown to select a patient

Alarm History

This app displays all of alarms that have gone off, from a specific device, over a certain amount of time. Once you open the app, two large boxes appear, the table on the left displays patient alarms and the table on the right displays technical alarms. The four column headings listed below are displayed (in both the patient alarms table and the technical alarms table) when running this app. You may sort the alarms by any of the headings by clicking on the heading itself. An arrow next to the heading will appear after clicking the title to show the direction that the data has been sorted (see image below for example). The length of each column may also be made longer or shorter by selecting the line in between each column and dragging it forwards or backwards. We believe that an app such as this one may help provide a better understanding of false and nuisance alarms through post hoc analysis.

Chart

This app uses line graphs to display vital signs data from various monitors. Each vital sign has one graph, and each graph may have several data points if the data is emitted from more than one monitor or device. If this is the case, each data point (or line) will be a different color, and a legend below the graph will state where the data is coming from. This data is displayed over a predetermined amount of time. This app allows the user to see the potential variability in vital signs data that comes from different monitors or devices.

Vital Signs
Line graphs of selected vital signs will display in this app.
  • To remove a vital sign from the app, click the red button to the right of the waveform graph.
  • To add a vital sign to the app, click the box with a down arrow next to “Add a vital sign”. Then select a vital sign and press the “Add” button.
Time Interval
At the bottom of the app there will be a blank box with a down arrow next to “Time Interval”. The following times are available: 10 seconds, 1 minute, 5 minutes, 15 minutes, 1 hour, 2 hours, 3 hours, 4 hours, 5 hours, and 6 hours.

Recommended devices for this application: all devices measuring vital signs.

Data Visualization

This app displays selected vital signs for one patient in the form of a shape. This allows the user to clearly see all vital signs and whether or not they are in the predetermined normal range. If three vital signs are selected (typically the default) then a triangle will appear (see Examples 1-3 below). If four vital signs are selected, then a square will appear (see Example 4). To add or delete a vital sign from the data visualization app, this vital sign must be added or deleted from the Infusion Safety app (see this section for details).

Each leg of the triangle will represent a different vital sign, and each line has markings with numeric values. The ends of the lines contain numbers outside of the normal range (for example: SpO2 greater than 100 or less than 95), and the middle of the lines contain numbers within the normal range (for example: SpO2 between 95 and 100).

The values that come from devices or physiological monitors are plotted on each line and are connected to one another, creating an inner shape. If there are more than two values for one vital sign, only the highest and the lowest values can be seen. This inner shape is colored based on the status of the vital sign data. To edit the values for when the inner shape will be green, yellow, or red, go to the Infusion Safety app section for more information.

Green
All vital signs in the normal range (see Examples 1 and 4).
Yellow
At least one vital sign is not in the normal range (see Example 2).
Red
Either one vital sign is severely abnormal, or more than one vital sign not in the normal range (see Example 3).

Recommended devices for this application: all devices measuring vital signs.

Infusion Safety

When this app is opened four text boxes will appear at the top of the screen.

Select Infusion Pump
Infusion pump connected from ICE Device Adapter will appear here.
Interlock Status
An alert will appear if the infusion is stopped. It will say why the pump was stopped (ex: low SpO2) and the time.
Infusion Status
States “select an infusion”.
Info. Messages
Will create a notification if a vital sign is low or high.
Respiration Rate, Heart Rate, and SpO2 will appear at the bottom of the page with colored bars below each.
  • The green color shows the safe data range.
  • The yellow color shows the potentially unsafe data range.
  • The red color shows the unsafe data range, which will cause an alarm on the app to sound.
The “Configuration Mode” is available to make edits to the app [listed below].
  • The number of warnings that become an alarm for a specific vital sign may be edited.
  • Choose to ignore zero for a specific vital sign.
  • A box may be checked off that makes a vital sign required for this app.
    • If this box is checked and there is no source of that vital sign then message will appear in the Informational Messages Box.

Patient ID

This application helps manage patients, devices and monitors. Specifically, patients may be connected to a device, and that patient may be selected while using other apps (and the devices selected in this app will be used). Patients can be connected to devices by selecting “Create an ICE Device Adapter”. Once a device is added it will automatically connect to the first name listed in the dropdown of patients listed at the bottom of the screen. To change the patient, click on the dropdown arrow before selecting the Create an ICE Device Adapter button. To disconnect the patient from the device in the application, click the button in the disconnect column. Once this has been done, the device will move to the box on the bottom right side of the page. To connect this device to another patient, click on a patient in the bottom left box and click on a device (both should be highlighted after they have been selected). Then press the button in between these two boxes.

To add a new patient to use in the OpenICE apps, click the button “Create new MRN entry”. A window will pop up to add a new patient. This window will give the following fields:

MRN
A medical record number is a unique identifier for each patient. This app will automatically create one. The MRN may be changed if desired.
First Name
Type in a first name.
Last Name
Type in a last name.
Gender
Select male or female dropdown list.
DOB
Type in a date of birth or click the icon to select a date from the calendar
All edits made while the app was running will be saved once the app is closed.
